## 4. Break■Even and Profitability Roadmap 
### 4.1 Understanding Break■Even Analysis 
The **breakeven point** is *“the exact level of sales where a company’s revenue equals its total expenses, meaning the business neither makes a profit nor has a 
loss”*■719684105307550†L256-L262■. It indicates how many units or how much revenue must be generated before the venture becomes profitable. The standard formula divides fixed costs by the contribution margin (selling price per unit minus variable cost per 
unit)■719684105307550†L270-L329■. Break■even analysis helps set pricing and sales targets and assesses financial viability■719684105307550†L286-L305■. 
### 4.2 Financial Assumptions and Scenarios 
Since the Omnistrux Meta is currently conceptual, the following numbers are illustrative. Actual figures will depend on precise designs, manufacturing costs and market conditions.
- **Fixed costs** (annual): administrative salaries, small office space, initial R&D, server costs – assume AU $1 million. - **Variable costs** per unit: cost to deliver each MVP (e.g., hardware kit manufacturing cost of AU $30, VR meditation distribution at AU $2 per download). For simplicity, assume an average variable cost of AU $40 per product unit across offerings. - **Selling price per unit**: premium due to the esoteric positioning; assume average sale price of AU $120 (with digital subscriptions and course fees bringing blended revenue). 
**Break■even calculation** (units): 
\[ \text{Break■even units} = \frac{\text{Fixed Costs}}{\text{Price per Unit} - \text{Variable Cost per Unit}} = \frac{1{,}000{,}000}{120 - 40} \approx 12{,}500 \text{ units}\, \] 
This means the business needs to sell roughly **12,500 units** across all products to cover AU $1 million in fixed costs at the given margins. If each unit is a subscription generating recurring revenue, the break■even point may be reached sooner. Once fixed costs are covered, each additional sale contributes directly to profit■719684105307550†L286-L305■.
